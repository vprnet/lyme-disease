Lyme Disease: The Quiet Epidemic

Forty years ago Lyme Disease didn't even have a name. Today, the neurodegenerative disease infects up to 300,000 annually and has greatly expanded its range beyond the small Connecticut town where it was first identified in 1975. While the data doesn't go that far back—and the CDC estimates that reported cases represent only 10% of the total number of infections—we are nonetheless left with an alarming picture of expanded range and skyrocketing infection rates over the last fifteen years. Nowhere is this expansion more evident than in Northern New England, home to the highest incident rates and fastest growing infection rates in the nation.
